Creative Process

According to the article How to Improve Creativity, there are about five stages in the creative process. Those five stages are Preparation, Incubation, Illumination, and Verification.

1. Preparation is when you start thinking of an idea and brainstorming. This is the stage of trying to find that spark of inspiration.

2. Incubation is the stage of taking a step back from the project at hand. In this stage you allow yourself to take a breather and not over think about the project. Stepping away from the project can allow you the space you need to let the creativity flow.

3. Illumination is the third stage, which is when everything clicks and you form a connection to make your idea work. This is the stage where your solution is found.

4. Evaluation stage is when you test the viability of your new idea. This is when you decide if it really is a good solution to your problem.

5. Verification, the last stage is when you get to work and bring your idea to life.
